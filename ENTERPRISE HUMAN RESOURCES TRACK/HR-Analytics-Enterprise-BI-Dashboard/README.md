An enterprise-grade Human Resources Tableau dashboard that profiles organizational health across 1,470 employees — delivering KPI summaries, attrition analysis, job satisfaction cross-tabulations, and department-level workforce insights.

Project Overview
Domain	Human Resources Analytics / Enterprise BI
Tools Used	Tableau
Dataset	HR Data.xlsx — 1,470 employee records
Key Files	HR Data.twbx, HR_Analytics_Tableau_Dashboard
Deliverable	Interactive enterprise Tableau HR Dashboard

Business Problem
•	HR leadership needed a single-pane view of workforce health — understanding attrition patterns, satisfaction levels, and department-level performance to make informed retention and hiring decisions.

What I Did
•	Engineered headline KPI metrics: Total Employees (1,470), Attrition Count (237), Attrition Rate (16.12%), Active Headcount (1,233), and Average Employee Age (37).: KPI Banner
•	Built a cross-tabulation chart mapping Job Satisfaction Ratings (1–4) against specific job roles — highlighting structural counts for Sales Executives (326) and Research Scientists (292).: Job Satisfaction Matrix
•	Visualized attrition breakdown by department, age group, gender, and education level to identify the highest exit risk segments.: Attrition Analysis
•	Created department-level filters allowing HR stakeholders to explore metrics for R&D, Sales, and HR divisions independently.: Department Drilldowns
•	Profiled the workforce age distribution across cohorts, revealing peak concentration in the 32–38 age bracket.: Age Distribution

Key Insights
•	The overall attrition rate of 16.12% indicates a moderate retention challenge requiring targeted HR interventions.
•	R&D Department has the highest absolute attrition count — a key area for retention focus.
•	Employees with Job Satisfaction rating of 1 show significantly higher attrition rates.
•	Sales Executives represent the largest job role group (326 employees), making them a priority for engagement programs.
