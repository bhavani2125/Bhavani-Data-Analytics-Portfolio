An interactive Tableau dashboard providing a comprehensive global view of Netflix's content library — mapping geographical distribution, content type splits, rating profiles, genre rankings, and production timelines across thousands of titles.

Project Overview
Domain	Media & Entertainment Analytics
Tools Used	Tableau
Dataset	Netflix_Titles.csv, netflix_data_cleaned.csv
Key Files	Netflix_Titles.twbx, Netflix_Titles_Tableau_Dashboard
Deliverable	Interactive multi-view Tableau BI Dashboard

What I Did
•	Engineered a dynamic world map visualization linking spatial coordinates to production volumes, revealing which countries produce the most Netflix content.: Global Choropleth Map
•	Instrumented a clear platform macro-split view: 5,185 Movies vs. 147 TV Shows across all content categories.: Content Type Split
•	Built a frequency bar chart identifying consumer segment patterns — peak concentrations at TV-MA (1,822 titles) and TV-14 (1,214 titles).: Rating Distribution Analysis
•	Constructed an interactive descending bar chart for Top 10 Netflix Genres — Dramas & International Movies led with 336 titles, followed by Stand-Up Comedy at 286.: Genre Ranking Dashboard
•	Developed an area trend chart tracking production history (1940–2021), clearly illustrating the explosive content growth post-2015 with micro-splits by content type.: Production Timeline

Key Insights
•	The United States produces the highest volume of Netflix content, followed by India and the United Kingdom.
•	TV-MA rated content dominates the library, reflecting Netflix's adult-audience focus.
•	Netflix content production increased by over 10x between 2010 and 2019.
•	International content (non-US) represents a growing share of the catalogue post-2016.
